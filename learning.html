CPU - Central Processing Unit

Think of the CPU as the computer's brain because it does all the thinking and problem-solving. It takes in information, makes quick decisions, and sends out commands so the computer knows exactly what to do next.

RAM - Random Access Memory

RAM is like your short-term memory because it holds onto the specific things you are thinking about right this second, like a math problem you are doing in your head. It helps the computer work fast, but just like a thought can pop out of your head, everything in RAM disappears the moment the computer turns off.

MBD - Motherboard

The motherboard is like your bones and nerves because it holds all the computer parts together and connects them so they can talk to each other.
Just like your bones give your body shape and your nerves carry messages from your brain, the motherboard keeps everything in place and has little electrical pathways to connect the parts.

PSU - Power Supply Unit

Like my creations need energy from food, the computer needs electricity as its food, which it gets from the PSU.

HDD - Hard Drive Disk

Just like you remember your birthday or a fun vacation from last year, the hard drive keeps all your photos, games, and drawings safe so they are still there when you turn the computer back on.

GPU - Graphics Processing Unit

Just like your eyes let you see the world in bright colors and moving shapes, the GPU works hard to draw all the pictures, movies, and cool explosions you see in your video games so they look real.
